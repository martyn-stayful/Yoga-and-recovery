Phase progression

Phase 1 · Days 1–7 · Gentle relief

Settling the protective bracing. Focus: lateral breathing, supported movement, restorative yoga, gentle TA activation.

Phase 2 · Days 8–14 · Controlled movement

Reintroducing rotation and extension within tolerance. Dead bugs and bird-dog prep, more active yoga, longer walks.

Phase 3 · Days 15–21 · Active stability

Building deep core endurance. Bird-dog, side plank prep, foam roller, strength-based vinyasa-lite.

Phase 4 · Days 22–28 · Progressive strength

Integrating strength under load. Full plank, side plank, loaded rotation, full vinyasa flow.

📋 How this programme is structured

Yoga is in the morning to mobilise after overnight stiffness. Tennis ball release, core work and walking sit through the day. Stretches are in the evening, and the heat pack before bed is the final calming input to settle the nervous system for sleep.

Daily check-ins: the morning check-in (after waking, before yoga) and evening check-in (before bed) take 30 seconds each. They're how the Progress tab learns what's working.

All progress saves automatically. Close the app and return any time — your ticks, ratings, current day, and streak will all be there.

🌅 Morning stiffness add-on (2 minutes in bed)

Before getting out of bed:

  • 10 slow lateral breaths (breathe wide into rib sides, not up)
  • Knees-to-chest, hold 30 seconds
  • Gentle knees side-to-side rocking, 10 each way

This warms the costovertebral joints before any load is placed on them.

🔥 Heat protocol

Heat is one of the most effective tools for chronic muscle bracing. It increases blood flow, reduces guarding, and calms the sympathetic nervous system.

  • Before bed (in the checklist): 15–20 mins on the mid-back, lying down
  • Optional second session: mid-afternoon if sitting has wound things up
  • Microwaveable wheat bag, hot water bottle, or electric pad — all fine
  • Always with a layer of fabric between heat and skin

⚠️ Flare day protocol

If pain flares or you've had a bad night, scale right back. Do only:

  • 5 minutes of lateral breathing (lying down)
  • 10-minute gentle walk
  • Heat pack before bed

Skip the yoga, stretches, tennis ball and core. Tick off what you do — partial counts. Still do the check-ins — flare data is useful data.

🎾 Tennis ball technique (mid-back)

Lie on the floor with one or two tennis balls placed either side of the spine (never directly on the spine itself) at the level of your bra strap or just below. Roll up to the base of the shoulder blades and back down slowly. Pause on tender spots for 20–30 seconds and breathe into them.

Spend 3–5 minutes total depending on phase. Stop earlier if it's reproducing the sharp pain — that's the joint, not muscle.

💪 Why core work matters here

The mid-back joints work harder when the deep core (transverse abdominis, obliques) isn't doing its job. The chronic muscle bracing you feel is partly the back compensating. As the deep core strengthens, the bracing reduces.

Quality over quantity — these are not ab workouts. The whole point is keeping the back quiet while the deep core fires.

💺 Sitting trigger management

If sitting aggravates it:

  • Set a timer for every 30 minutes — stand and do 5 lateral breaths
  • Roll a small towel and place it horizontally behind the mid-back at desk
  • Avoid the "C" slumped shape — bring chest tall, ribs over hips

🌬️ Hourly breathing reset (do this all day)

Once an hour: 5 slow lateral breaths. 30 seconds. Mobilises the costovertebral joints continuously and prevents the bracing from re-locking.

🛏️ Sleep position

Side-lying with a pillow between knees, hugging a second pillow at chest height (keeps the rib cage open). Avoid sleeping on your front.

🚨 When to see a professional

Talk to a GP or physiotherapist if you experience:

  • Pain radiating around the rib cage to the front
  • Pain with deep breaths that's getting worse, not better
  • Numbness, tingling, or weakness anywhere
  • No improvement after 2 weeks of consistent work
  • Sharp pain that's increasing in frequency or intensity

This programme supports professional care — it doesn't replace it.
